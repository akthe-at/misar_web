About Our Team

Team Qualifications

Our deployable and certified team members undergo extensive training and testing to ensure proficiency in search and rescue operations.
Minimum qualifications and frequent training exercises focus on:

  • First Aid/CPR
  • Map and Compass
  • Radio Communications
  • Bloodborne Pathogen Training
  • ICS Certs: IS-100, IS-200, IS-700 & IS-800
  • Crime Scene Preservation
  • Lost Person Behavior
  • Land Navigation Certification:
  • (MRA105 Operations Level/SARTECH II)
  • Hazardous Materials Awareness
  • K9 First Aid

Why Dogs?

Dogs play a crucial role in search and rescue operations and bring many unique abilities as search assets:

Sense of smell

Dogs can detect the scent of a missing person from a considerable distance even through obstacles like bushes.

Agility

Their physical agility allows them to navigate rough and dangerous terrain, and reach areas that might be inaccesible to humans like thick vegetation.

Coverage

One dog can cover the same search area as numerous human searchers, making them highly efficient.

Endurance

Dogs possess high levels of endurance and can maintain search efforts for extended periods

Working Drive

The strong work ethic and enthusiasm turn a search mission into a game for the dog that they love, driving them to perform at their very best

Collaborative Effort

Training a search dog is a team effort. The process involves many individuals dedicating countless hours and trainings in most weather conditions imaginable. This collective dedication is vital for developing a proficient team capabile of aiding in search and rescue missions in a large assortment of environmental conditions and regardless of the time of day.

Partnerships with Law Enforcement

Our team works closely with law enforcement agencies to assist in search and rescue operations. We provide our services and expertise to help find missing persons, offering vital support to both the law enforcement agencies and the impacted families.

Volunteer Services

Our services are provided entirely by volunteers and are always free of charge to both law enforcement agencies and families in need. Our commitment is to serve the community without any financial burden, ensuring that our services are available to all who require it.

People have said to me from time to time things like “lighten up, it’s just a dog” or “that’s a lot of money for just a dog”. They say to me they don’t understand the distance traveled, the time spent, or the costs involved for just a dog. Some of my proudest moments have come about with just a dog. Many hours have passed and the only company I had was just a dog but I did not once feel slighted. Some of my saddest moments have been brought about by just a dog, and in those days of darkness, the gentle touch of just a dog gave me comfort and reason to overcome the day.


For those of you who do not understand the whys and wherefores of just a dog, then you will probably understand phases like “just a friend”, “just a sunrise”, or “just a promise”. Just a dog brings into my life the very essence of friendship, trust, and pure unbridled joy. Just a dog brings out in me the compassion and patience that make me a better person. Because of just a dog I will rise early, take long walks and look longingly to the future. So for me and folks like me, it’s not just a dog but an embodiment of all the hopes and dreams of the future, the fond memories of the past, and the pure joy of the moment. Just a dog brings out what’s good in me and diverts my thoughts away from myself and the worries of the day.


I hope that someday they can understand that it’s not just a dog but the thing that gives me humanity and keeps me from being just a man. So the next time you hear the phrase just a dog, just smile, because they just don’t understand.


Just a thought,


-Richard Biby
